# Open Heart Watch Parts

## What is an Open Heart Watch?
An open heart watch has a **window/cutout in the dial** that shows the balance wheel oscillating underneath. It's different from:
- **Skeleton watch** ‚Äî entire movement visible through the dial (full cutouts)
- **Open heart** ‚Äî small window showing just the balance wheel (partial cutout)

The open heart gives a peek at the mechanical movement without exposing everything. It's a popular mid-range luxury feature (Tissot, Hamilton, Orient all make open heart watches).

---

## Movement: NH72 (Skeleton Platform)
The NH72 is the same as NH35 but with a skeletonized balance wheel bridge ‚Äî designed to be seen. The NH35's balance wheel is covered; the NH72's is exposed for open heart/skeleton dials.

**Use NH72, NOT NH35 for open heart builds.**
